What Is Emergency Tree Service?

Emergency tree service covers any situation where a tree or large branch poses an immediate threat to people, property, or infrastructure. This includes trees that have fallen onto homes, vehicles, or power lines, as well as trees that are actively leaning, cracking, or uprooting and could fail at any moment. In Lake Charles, these emergencies are most common during and after hurricanes, tropical storms, and severe thunderstorms, but they can happen on a calm day when a weakened tree finally gives out.

Our emergency crews are available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. When you call, we assess the urgency of your situation and dispatch a team as quickly as possible. We arrive with chainsaws, rigging equipment, and the heavy machinery needed to safely remove fallen trees from structures, clear blocked roads and driveways, and eliminate active hazards. Safety is our first priority, both for your family and our crew.

We also coordinate directly with insurance companies to make the claims process easier on you. Our team documents the damage with photographs, provides written assessments, and can supply itemized invoices that meet the requirements of most homeowner insurance policies. We have been through this process hundreds of times and know exactly what the adjusters need.

When Do You Need Emergency Tree Service?

If any of the following situations apply, do not wait to call. Tree emergencies can escalate quickly, especially during active weather:

  • A tree has fallen on your home, garage, carport, or other structure
  • A tree or large branch is resting on power lines or has pulled lines down
  • A tree has fallen across your driveway, street, or access road
  • A large tree is visibly cracking, splitting, or leaning suddenly
  • Storm damage has left a tree partially uprooted with exposed roots
  • A tree has fallen on a vehicle, fence, or other property
  • You hear cracking sounds from a large tree during or after a storm

While you wait for our crew to arrive, keep everyone away from the damaged tree and any downed power lines. Do not attempt to cut or move the tree yourself. If power lines are involved, call your utility company in addition to calling us. Our team will handle the tree safely once we are on site.
