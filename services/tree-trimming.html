What Is Professional Tree Trimming?

Professional tree trimming is the selective removal of branches to improve a tree's health, structure, and appearance. Unlike DIY pruning with a pair of loppers, professional trimming follows arboricultural standards that promote strong growth patterns while reducing the risk of branch failure. In Lake Charles, where high winds and heavy rain are a fact of life, properly trimmed trees are significantly more resistant to storm damage.

Our crews perform several types of trimming depending on your trees' needs. Crown thinning opens the canopy to allow wind to pass through rather than catching it like a sail. Crown raising removes lower branches to provide clearance over driveways, sidewalks, and structures. Deadwood removal targets dead, dying, or diseased branches before they fall on their own. Each cut is made at the proper location to encourage healthy wound closure and prevent decay from entering the trunk.

Regular trimming is one of the most effective ways to extend the life of your trees and protect your property. Trees that are trimmed on a consistent schedule develop stronger branch unions, maintain balanced canopies, and are far less likely to drop large limbs during storms. It also keeps your yard looking well-maintained and can increase your property's curb appeal and value.

When Do You Need Tree Trimming?

Knowing when to call for trimming can save you from costly damage and keep your trees in peak condition. Schedule a trimming service when you notice:

  • Branches are growing over your roof, gutters, or power lines
  • Dead or broken limbs are visible in the canopy
  • The tree's canopy has become thick, dense, or lopsided
  • Low-hanging branches block walkways, driveways, or sightlines
  • Branches are rubbing against each other and creating weak points
  • You want to prepare your trees for hurricane season
  • It has been more than two years since the tree was last trimmed

The best time to trim most hardwood trees in Louisiana is during the dormant season from late fall through early spring, though deadwood and hazard branches can be removed any time of year. We recommend scheduling your trimming before hurricane season begins in June to give your trees the best chance of weathering severe storms.

Our Approach to Tree Trimming

Proper tree trimming is both an art and a science. Done correctly, trimming improves a tree's structure, reduces wind resistance during storms, removes dead or diseased wood, and enhances the natural form of the tree. Done incorrectly — with improper cuts, excessive topping, or the wrong timing — trimming can stress a tree and create entry points for disease.

Our ISA-certified arborists make trimming decisions based on tree biology, not just aesthetics. We identify which branches are structurally weak, which are crossing and causing damage, and which dead wood poses a fall risk. The result is a tree that looks better, grows healthier, and is better prepared to handle Southwest Louisiana's storm seasons.
