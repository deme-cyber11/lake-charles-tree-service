What Is Storm Damage Cleanup?

Storm damage cleanup is the process of removing fallen trees, broken branches, and scattered debris from your property after a hurricane, tropical storm, or severe thunderstorm. Lake Charles sits in one of the most hurricane-prone corridors in the United States, and residents know all too well the damage that high winds and heavy rain can inflict on the local tree canopy. After a major storm, properties can be buried under mountains of limbs, trunks, and vegetation that need to be cleared before life can return to normal.

Our storm cleanup service covers the full scope of post-storm work. We remove fallen trees from yards, driveways, and structures. We cut and haul broken branches and scattered debris. We assess standing trees for hidden damage that could cause them to fail in the next storm. And we provide complete insurance documentation so you can file your claim with confidence. Our goal is to get your property safe, accessible, and clean as quickly as possible.

Having been through multiple hurricane seasons serving the Lake Charles community, our crews are experienced with large-scale storm response. We understand the urgency, we have the equipment, and we know how to prioritize jobs so that the most dangerous situations are handled first while still getting to every customer who needs help.

When Do You Need Storm Damage Cleanup?

After any significant weather event in the Lake Charles area, take a careful look at your property. You need professional storm cleanup when:

  • Trees or large branches have fallen in your yard, on structures, or across driveways
  • Broken limbs are hanging in the canopy and could fall at any time
  • Debris is blocking your driveway, walkways, or access to your home
  • A tree has been partially uprooted and is leaning at a new angle
  • Scattered branches and vegetation cover your lawn, garden beds, or roof
  • You need documentation for an insurance claim related to storm damage
  • Standing trees show new cracks, splits, or bark separation after the storm

It is important to act quickly after a storm. Damaged trees that are still standing can fall without warning, especially if additional rain loosens the soil around compromised root systems. Clearing debris promptly also prevents secondary issues like mold growth under piled vegetation and pest infestations in decaying wood.

Our Approach to Storm Damage Cleanup

After a Gulf hurricane or severe thunderstorm, the scale of debris can feel overwhelming. Fallen trees, broken limbs, scattered wood — it accumulates fast and creates safety hazards that need to be addressed before normal life can resume. Our storm damage cleanup service is built for exactly this situation.
