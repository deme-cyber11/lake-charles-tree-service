How Much Does Tree Service Cost in Lake Charles?

March 15, 2026 5 min read

If you're a homeowner in Lake Charles or anywhere in Calcasieu Parish, understanding tree service costs helps you budget properly and avoid overpaying. Whether you need a dead oak removed from your backyard or routine trimming to keep your property looking sharp, here's a detailed breakdown of what tree services cost in our area.

Tree Removal Costs in Lake Charles

Tree removal is typically the most expensive tree service because of the labor, equipment, and risk involved. In the Lake Charles area, tree removal costs generally range from $350 to $2,200, depending on the size and complexity of the job.

Small trees under 30 feet tall, such as young pines or ornamental trees, usually cost between $350 and $600 to remove. Medium-sized trees between 30 and 60 feet, which includes many of the water oaks and sweetgums common throughout Southwest Louisiana, typically run $600 to $1,200. Large trees over 60 feet, such as mature live oaks or tall loblolly pines, can cost $1,200 to $2,200 or more.

These prices generally include cutting the tree down, removing the trunk and branches, and hauling away the debris. Stump removal is usually quoted separately.

Tree Trimming and Pruning Costs

Regular trimming is essential for tree health and property safety, especially in a hurricane-prone region like Lake Charles. Tree trimming costs in our area typically range from $150 to $800 per tree.

Smaller trees and basic trimming jobs fall on the lower end, while large trees that require climbing or bucket truck access run higher. Crown thinning for mature oaks, a popular service for storm preparation, usually costs between $400 and $800. If you have multiple trees that need trimming at the same time, many companies offer discounted package pricing.

Stump Grinding Costs

After a tree is removed, you're left with a stump that can attract termites, create tripping hazards, and make mowing difficult. Stump grinding in Lake Charles costs between $100 and $400 per stump.

The price depends primarily on the diameter of the stump and the root system. A small stump under 12 inches might cost $100 to $150, while a large stump from a mature oak could run $250 to $400. Most tree service companies offer a discount when you combine stump grinding with tree removal.

Factors That Affect Tree Service Pricing

Several factors can push the cost of tree service up or down. Understanding these variables helps you anticipate what your specific job might cost.

Tree size and species are the biggest factors. Taller, wider trees require more labor, larger equipment, and more time. Hardwood species like live oak are denser and heavier than softwoods like pine, which means more work to cut and haul away.

Location and accessibility play a major role. A tree standing in an open yard is far easier and cheaper to remove than one wedged between your house and a fence line. Trees near power lines, structures, or other obstacles require specialized rigging techniques that increase the cost.

Tree condition matters as well. Dead, diseased, or storm-damaged trees can be unpredictable and dangerous to work on, which may increase the price. Conversely, a healthy tree in good condition is more straightforward to trim or remove.

Emergency service commands a premium. If a tree falls on your home during a storm and needs immediate removal, expect to pay more than you would for a scheduled job. Emergency tree service often involves after-hours work, expedited response, and hazardous conditions. That said, getting a dangerous tree handled quickly is always worth the investment in your family's safety.

Insurance Coverage for Storm Damage

Living in Lake Charles means dealing with hurricanes, tropical storms, and severe weather. The good news is that homeowner's insurance often covers tree removal when a tree falls on a structure like your house, garage, or fence. Most policies cover the cost of removing the tree from the structure and may also cover debris removal up to a certain limit.

However, insurance typically does not cover removing a tree that fell in your yard without hitting a structure. It also usually does not cover preventive tree removal or routine maintenance. We recommend reviewing your policy and talking to your insurance agent before storm season so you know exactly what's covered.

Tips for Getting the Best Value

Getting quality tree service at a fair price comes down to a few smart strategies. First, get multiple estimates. A reputable tree service company will provide a free, no-obligation estimate after inspecting the job in person. Comparing two or three quotes helps you understand the fair market rate for your specific job.

Second, plan ahead when possible. Scheduling tree work during the off-season or combining multiple jobs into one visit can often save you money. Emergency service is always more expensive than planned work.

Third, verify credentials. Always hire a licensed and insured tree service company. The cheapest quote is not always the best value. An uninsured crew that damages your property or gets injured on your land can end up costing you far more than you saved.

Finally, ask about bundling. If you need several trees trimmed or want stump grinding included with a removal, ask about package pricing. Most professional tree service companies are happy to offer a better rate for larger jobs.
