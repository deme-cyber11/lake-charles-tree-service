How to Choose a Tree Service Contractor

March 15, 2026 5 min read

Hiring the wrong tree service contractor can lead to property damage, personal injury, and unexpected costs. Tree work is inherently dangerous, requiring specialized equipment, training, and insurance. Whether you need a tree removed, trimmed, or treated for disease, choosing the right contractor protects your property, your wallet, and your peace of mind. Here is what Lake Charles homeowners should look for when selecting a professional tree service company.

Verify Licensing and Credentials

The first thing to check before hiring any tree service company is whether they hold the proper licenses to operate in Louisiana and your local municipality. In Lake Charles and Calcasieu Parish, tree service contractors should carry a valid state contractor's license and any required local business permits.

Beyond basic licensing, look for industry credentials that demonstrate professional knowledge. Arborists certified by the International Society of Arboriculture (ISA) have passed rigorous testing on tree biology, diagnosis, maintenance practices, and safety. While certification is not legally required, it shows that the company invests in professional development and follows industry best practices. A company that takes the time to earn and maintain professional credentials is far more likely to deliver quality work.

Confirm Insurance Coverage

Insurance is non-negotiable when hiring a tree service contractor. This is perhaps the single most important item to verify before any work begins. A legitimate tree service company should carry both general liability insurance and workers' compensation insurance.

General liability insurance protects your property if something goes wrong during the job. If a tree falls on your fence, a branch damages your neighbor's car, or equipment tears up your lawn, the contractor's liability policy covers the repairs. Without it, you could be personally liable for the damage.

Workers' compensation insurance protects you if a crew member is injured on your property. Tree work is one of the most dangerous occupations in the country. If an uninsured worker falls from a tree or is struck by a limb on your property, you could face a lawsuit for medical expenses and lost wages. Always ask to see a current certificate of insurance and verify it is active by calling the insurance company directly. A reputable contractor will have no problem providing this documentation.

Look for Experience and Track Record

Experience matters in tree service. A company that has been operating in the Lake Charles area for several years understands the local tree species, soil conditions, weather patterns, and common problems specific to Southwest Louisiana. They know how live oaks respond to pruning, which pine species are prone to beetle infestations, and how to prepare trees for hurricane season.

Ask how long the company has been in business and what types of jobs they handle most frequently. A well-established company with a proven track record in your community is a safer bet than a new operation with no local references. Check online reviews on Google, Facebook, and other platforms to see what previous customers have to say about the quality of work, professionalism, and reliability.

Get Free Written Estimates

A professional tree service company should provide free, detailed estimates before starting any work. The estimate should be based on an in-person inspection of the job, not a phone quote or sight-unseen guess. A reputable contractor will visit your property, assess the trees, discuss your options, and provide a written quote that clearly outlines the scope of work and the total cost.

Be cautious of any company that provides a quote over the phone without seeing the trees, asks for full payment upfront before work begins, or pressures you into signing a contract on the spot. These are common tactics used by fly-by-night operations and storm chasers who pass through the area after severe weather. Get estimates from at least two or three companies so you can compare pricing, scope of work, and professionalism.

Ask for References

Any tree service company worth hiring should be able to provide references from recent customers in the Lake Charles area. Ask for the names and contact information of at least two or three homeowners who had similar work done. Call those references and ask about their experience: Was the crew professional and punctual? Did they complete the work as described in the estimate? Was the cleanup thorough? Were there any unexpected charges?

In addition to direct references, look at the company's online presence. A well-maintained website, active social media profiles, and a strong collection of positive reviews all indicate a company that takes its reputation seriously. Companies that do good work consistently are not hard to find online.

Evaluate Equipment and Safety Practices

Professional tree service requires specialized equipment, including chainsaws, chippers, stump grinders, bucket trucks, cranes, and rigging hardware. A company that shows up with a pickup truck and a chainsaw may not have the tools needed to handle your job safely and efficiently.

Ask about the company's safety practices and training programs. Professional crews should wear personal protective equipment, including hard hats, eye protection, hearing protection, and chaps or leg protection when operating chainsaws. They should follow ANSI Z133 safety standards for arboricultural operations. If a crew shows up without basic safety gear, that is a significant red flag about how they approach their work overall.

Also pay attention to how the crew handles the job site. Professional tree service companies protect your lawn and landscaping, set up traffic cones or barriers when working near roads, and leave your property clean when the job is complete. The quality of the cleanup often reflects the quality of the tree work itself.

Value Local Knowledge

A tree service company with deep local roots in Lake Charles and Calcasieu Parish brings valuable knowledge that out-of-town operators simply do not have. Local companies understand the specific challenges of this region: the heavy clay soils that affect root stability, the mix of hardwood and softwood species throughout the area, the intense hurricane seasons, and the regulatory requirements for tree work near utility lines or in flood zones.

Local companies also have a reputation to maintain in the community. They depend on word-of-mouth referrals and repeat business from Lake Charles homeowners. That built-in accountability means they are far more likely to stand behind their work, address any issues promptly, and treat your property with care.

Red Flags to Watch For

Knowing what to avoid is just as important as knowing what to look for. Here are warning signs that a tree service contractor may not be trustworthy:

  • Door-to-door solicitation immediately after a storm, especially from out-of-state crews with no local presence.
  • Cash-only payment with no written estimate, contract, or receipt.
  • No insurance documentation or refusal to provide proof of coverage.
  • Extremely low bids that are far below other estimates, which often indicates corner-cutting on safety or insurance.
  • Topping trees as a recommended practice. Topping is widely recognized as harmful and is condemned by every major arboricultural organization. A company that suggests topping does not understand proper tree care.
  • Demanding full payment upfront before any work has started.
